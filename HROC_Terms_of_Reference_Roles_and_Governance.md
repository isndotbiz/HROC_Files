1. Overview

This Terms of Reference (TOR) establishes the governance framework, roles and responsibilities of the Board of Directors, Executive Committee, and Standing Committees for Healing Roots Outreach Collective (HROC). It ensures clear accountability, regulatory compliance under Washington law (RCW 24.03A), and alignment with nonprofit best practices.  ￼

⸻

2. Purpose

To formalize governance structures and clarify expectations for board members and officers, ensuring leadership integrity, fiduciary accountability, program efficacy, and ethical operations within HROC.

⸻

3. Governance Structure

3.1 Board of Directors
	•	Composition: Three voting members – Jonathan Mallinger (Chair/Secretary); Lilly Fedas (Treasurer); Brianna Bear (Equity & Cultural Advisor).
Additional non-voting advisory seats may include community or tribal elders.
	•	Term: 3-year term, renewable once (maximum two consecutive terms), in line with Leading with Intent governance norms.  ￼
	•	Meetings: Quarterly in-person or virtual. Special meetings may occur as needed.
	•	Quorum: A majority of voting members (2 of 3) is required for valid decisions.  ￼

3.2 Executive Committee

All officers, serving between board meetings to manage urgent governance matters:
	•	Chairperson (Jonathan): Facilitates board meetings, external representation, strategic liaison, CEO oversight.  ￼ ￼
	•	Vice Chairperson: Supports Chair and fills in absent Chair duties, supports governance flow.
	•	Secretary (Jonathan): Maintains official records, meeting minutes, and notice compliance.
	•	Treasurer (Lilly): Oversees budgets, financial reports, compliance, and internal controls.

⸻

4. Director & Officer Roles

4.1 Chairperson
	•	Lead board meetings and facilitate full engagement.
	•	Cultivate board-staff partnership, especially with CEO/executive director.  ￼ ￼ ￼ ￼
	•	Monitor adherence to mission, bylaws, and nonprofit law.
	•	Undertake community relations and high-level strategic dialogues.

4.2 Vice Chairperson
	•	Lead in Chairperson’s absence.
	•	Assist with committee oversight and onboarding new board members.

4.3 Secretary
	•	Maintain corporate records and minutes.
	•	Ensure timely board meeting and annual filings.

4.4 Treasurer
	•	Oversee finance committee and ensure reports are timely.
	•	Monitor budget vs. actuals, cash flow, grant spending.
	•	Lead audit readiness and adherence to 2 CFR §200 accounting standards.  ￼ ￼

⸻

5. Committees

5.1 Standing Committees

(In alignment with BoardSource governance frameworks)  ￼
	•	Finance Committee: Oversees budgeting, financial risk, audit prep.
	•	Governance & Nominating Committee: Manages recruitment, Board evaluation, nominations and term limits.
	•	Program Committee: Reviews outreach effectiveness, strategic planning, program quality.

5.2 Ad Hoc Committees

Established by the board for specific tasks or short-term needs (e.g., a capital campaign, special hiring), reporting back to the board with recommendations.

⸻

6. Decision‑Making Process
	1.	Proposal Development
Concept and draft resolutions originate from staff or committees.
	2.	Committee Review
Governance or Program Committee vets proposals for fit and resource alignment.
	3.	Board Discussion
Major proposals introduced under “New Business,” debated, and amended as needed.
	4.	Vote & Decision
Passing requires majority vote of board members present (quorum).
	5.	Implementation
Responsible party is charged with execution, reporting back on results.

⸻

7. Conflict Resolution & Ethics
	•	All board members must annually sign the Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement and recuse from conflicted discussions.  ￼
	•	Governance Committee leads mediation or formal resolution of conflicts.
	•	Irremediable disputes may be escalated to an external facilitator or consultant at board cost.

⸻

8. Performance Review
	•	Every two years, the board shall conduct a self-assessment, facilitated by the Governance Committee.
	•	Annual policy review occurs each July to ensure currency and alignment with best practices.  ￼ ￼ ￼

⸻

9. Fiduciary Duties

Board and officers owe duties of care, loyalty, and obedience, ensuring prudent leadership, mission-first decisions, and legal compliance.  ￼

⸻

10. Orientation & Onboarding

New board members receive a full orientation packet including:
	•	Mission, values, bylaws, and core policies
	•	Biographies of fellow board members and senior staff
	•	Financial documents and recent meeting minutes
	•	Form 1023 exemption letter and 501(c)(3) tax status
	•	Board member agreement outlining expectations.  ￼ ￼
