Our Impact Stories

Real stories of healing, connection, and transformation happening in our communities every day

Our mobile RV brings life-saving services directly to communities. Meeting people where they are with dignity, compassion, and peer support.

We provide naloxone distribution, syringe services, wound care, and crisis navigation in neighborhoods across King and Pierce Counties. Our outreach team works 3-5 days per week, building relationships and providing essential harm reduction supplies without judgment. Every visit is an opportunity to connect people with resources and support on their healing journey.

Since launching mobile outreach, we've distributed over 10,000 units of harm reduction supplies and provided direct services to more than 2,000 individuals. Our commitment is unwavering: no one deserves to suffer alone, and everyone deserves access to compassionate, evidence-based care regardless of their circumstances.

Building relationships based on trust, respect, and genuine care. Our peer-led approach centers the voices of those with lived experience.

We believe that people with lived experience of substance use and housing instability are the best leaders and advocates. Our team brings authentic understanding of the challenges our communities face. By leading with respect and cultural humility, we've built deep trust with unhoused and street-involved individuals who know they can rely on us for judgment-free care and support. This trust is the foundation of all meaningful change.

Trust is earned through consistency and demonstrated care over time. Our team members are visible in communities week after week, remembering names, learning stories, and following through on commitments. People know they can trust us because we show up, listen without judgment, and prioritize their dignity and autonomy in every interaction.

Peer support changes lives. Our lived-experience navigators provide crisis support, treatment referrals, and authentic connection on the path to healing.

Every person deserves someone who gets it. Our peer navigators have walked similar paths and understand the barriers to treatment, housing, and health care. They provide one-on-one support, crisis de-escalation, and connections to medical and mental health services. Through genuine relationship building, we help people navigate complex systems and find hope. We've seen people stabilize housing, access treatment, and reclaim their lives through consistent, caring peer support.

The power of peer support lies in lived experience and authentic connection. When someone struggling with addiction or homelessness meets a navigator who has been there and recovered, it transforms possibility into reality. Hope becomes concrete, and change becomes achievable.

About Us

Our Mission

Healing Roots Outreach Collective provides Indigenous-informed, peer-led mobile harm reduction services to unhoused and street-involved individuals in King and Pierce Counties, Washington.

We deliver life-saving naloxone distribution, sterile syringe services, wound care, peer-based crisis navigation, and cultural wellness support directly to communities with limited access to healthcare.

Our trauma-informed approach centers healing, recognizes historical and ongoing trauma, and honors Indigenous knowledge and cultural practices.
