üíª Technology as Liberation

Jonathan sees technology not as a replacement for human connection, but as a force multiplier for community impact. He's built custom systems that help peer workers track supplies without invasive surveillance, data dashboards that tell stories rather than reduce lives to numbers, and communication tools that preserve anonymity while fostering belonging.

He proved that you don't have to sacrifice dignity for accountability - and that better technology creates better outcomes for everyone.

Jonathan's approach to technology is rooted in a simple but radical principle: tools should empower communities, not extract data from them. Every line of code, every database design, every interface carries this commitment. Technology becomes liberation when it's designed with - not for - the people it serves.

Jonathan believes that sustainable change requires more than good intentions - it requires intelligent design, thoughtful infrastructure, and systems that work with human nature rather than against it. When systems are designed well, people can focus on what matters most: showing up for each other with compassion, consistency, and care.
