Mobile Outreach Unit

Bringing comprehensive harm reduction services directly to community neighborhoods

What We Provide

Barriers to accessing services are significant for people experiencing homelessness and substance use. Transportation, office locations in unfamiliar neighborhoods, inflexible hours, and fear of institutional settings prevent people from seeking help. Healing Roots Outreach Collective operates a mobile outreach unit that brings our full spectrum of services directly to people‚Äîin their neighborhoods, at their convenience, without barriers. Our mobile unit is a physical symbol that services meet people where they are, literally and figuratively.

Our mobile outreach unit travels throughout King and Pierce Counties on a regular schedule, stopping in neighborhoods where people experiencing homelessness gather‚Äîparks, bus stations, known encampments, and community locations. At each stop, our team provides every service we offer: naloxone distribution and overdose training, syringe exchange and hepatitis screening, wound care and health assessments, peer counseling, resource navigation, benefits assistance, and community education. Someone can access comprehensive services from a single unit without traveling to multiple locations or dealing with multiple systems.

The mobile unit is staffed by peer specialists and trained health workers who know the community and have relationships with the people we serve. Unlike some mobile services that feel clinical and intimidating, our unit is welcoming, warm, and centered on human connection. We remember people's names, their stories, their goals, and their progress. Over time, these relationships build trust. People come to know they won't be judged, that their needs will be taken seriously, and that we genuinely care about their wellbeing. Trust is the foundation of all our work.

The mobile unit also serves as a safe space. In winter, people can come in to warm up. In summer, they can find shade and cool air. We provide coffee, snacks, and sometimes meals. We listen to what people need and help them access resources. We're also a visible presence in the community‚Äîdemonstrating that homelessness and substance use are public health issues that deserve public health responses. Our visibility helps normalize harm reduction and builds community support for services.

Scheduling and location information for the mobile unit is available through contact information. We also respond to requests from communities wanting our services. If you know of an area that would benefit from mobile outreach services, please let us know. Our goal is to reach everyone in King and Pierce Counties who needs our services, whether they're in Seattle neighborhoods, suburban areas, or more rural communities.

Program Components

  • Comprehensive Services: All HROC services available from the mobile unit
  • Regular Schedule: Predictable stops in different neighborhoods throughout the week
  • Peer-Led Team: Trained peer specialists provide welcoming, culturally-informed care
  • Flexible Hours: Services available at times convenient for people on the street
  • Safe Space: Warm shelter, seating, beverages, and basic necessities
  • Relationship-Centered: Building ongoing relationships and trust with people served
  • No Barriers: No appointments, paperwork, or prerequisites required
  • Community Outreach: Request services for your neighborhood or event

How to Access Services

The mobile unit operates 3-5 days per week throughout King and Pierce Counties. Contact us for the current schedule and locations. You can visit the unit at any stop without an appointment. If you represent a community location or organization interested in hosting the mobile unit, we welcome collaboration. All services at the mobile unit are free, confidential, and judgment-free.
