Overdose Prevention

Life-saving naloxone distribution, fentanyl testing, and overdose response training

What We Provide

Overdose remains one of the leading causes of death in our region, particularly among individuals experiencing homelessness and substance use. Healing Roots Outreach Collective is committed to preventing overdose deaths through accessible, judgment-free distribution of life-saving naloxone (Narcan) and comprehensive overdose response training.

Our Overdose Prevention program puts powerful tools directly into the hands of people who need them most. We distribute naloxone kits at no cost, along with detailed instructions on how to recognize overdose signs and respond effectively. We've witnessed the direct impact of rapid naloxone administration‚Äîpeople who would have died are alive today because someone in their community had access to this medication and the knowledge to use it.

Fentanyl has dramatically increased overdose risk across all substances. Our fentanyl test strips help people make informed decisions about substance use safety. Combined with naloxone distribution and training, these tools create multiple layers of protection against overdose death. Our peer outreach workers don't just hand out supplies‚Äîwe build relationships, answer questions, and ensure people feel supported in harm reduction practices.

Overdose prevention is peer-led prevention. When trained peer navigators distribute naloxone and answer questions about fentanyl, it removes stigma and builds trust. People are more likely to carry naloxone, use it correctly, and call for help if they know they won't be judged. This is how we save lives‚Äîthrough accessible, non-judgmental, culturally-informed harm reduction.

Since we launched our program, we have distributed over 5,000 naloxone kits and trained more than 1,500 community members in overdose response. Each kit represents a life saved, a family kept together, a person given another chance at recovery and healing.

Overdose prevention is part of a comprehensive approach to harm reduction. It works alongside syringe services, wound care, peer support, and healthcare navigation to meet people where they are with dignity and evidence-based care.

Program Components

  • Naloxone Distribution: Free naloxone kits with detailed instructions
  • Overdose Response Training: Community education on recognizing and responding to overdose
  • Fentanyl Test Strips: No-cost fentanyl testing strips for harm reduction
  • Peer Support: Compassionate guidance from lived-experience peer counselors
  • Healthcare Linkage: Connection to treatment and medical services
  • Ongoing Education: Regular updates on fentanyl risks and overdose safety

How to Access Services

Our mobile outreach team distributes naloxone and provides overdose prevention training 3-5 days per week throughout King and Pierce Counties. Contact us to schedule a visit or learn about upcoming training sessions. Services are free, confidential, and judgment-free.
